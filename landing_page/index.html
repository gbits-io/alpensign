The Problem

Banks lose billions to fraud ‚Äî and their only evidence is their own logs.

When a client disputes a payment, the bank's defense rests on server logs they generated, stored, and control. That's self-serving evidence. Courts know it. Regulators know it. Fraudsters know it.

Self-Serving Evidence

Bank logs prove what the bank says happened. In a dispute, that's structurally weak ‚Äî the bank is both the record keeper and the interested party.

üí∏

Friendly Fraud

Clients who made legitimate purchases then dispute them. Buyer's remorse disguised as fraud. Banks absorb billions annually because they can't prove the client was present.

Weak Authentication Proof

SMS OTP, push notifications, app-based 2FA ‚Äî all controlled by the bank or dependent on Apple and Google infrastructure. No independent, verifiable trail.

Burden of Proof Stuck with the Bank

In disputes, banks must prove the client authorized the transaction. With only self-serving logs, that's an uphill battle. AlpenSign's independent cryptographic evidence enables a potential reversal of the burden of proof ‚Äî the client would need to explain away an immutable, biometric-confirmed seal.

AlpenSign creates an independent evidence chain ‚Äî from verified hardware through verified identity to immutable proof of authorization.

Regulatory Compliance & Alignment

Designed with European regulation in mind.

AlpenSign's architecture aligns with existing and emerging regulatory frameworks for electronic signatures, data protection, and financial services compliance.

‚úçÔ∏è

Comparable to Qualified Electronic Signatures

AlpenSign seals combine biometric confirmation, hardware-isolated key storage, and a secure element ‚Äî the same building blocks underlying QES under the EU's eIDAS regulation. While not a certified QES provider, the cryptographic guarantees are structurally equivalent: identity-bound, non-repudiable, and tamper-evident.

üîí

GDPR-Aligned by Design

No personal data is stored on-chain. Transaction seals contain only cryptographic hashes ‚Äî no names, account numbers, or payment details touch the Solana ledger. The biometric confirmation stays entirely on-device within the Seeker's secure element, never transmitted or stored externally.

üìú

Immutable Audit Trail

Every seal creates a timestamped, cryptographically signed record on Solana's public ledger ‚Äî an independent audit trail that neither the bank nor the client can alter after the fact. This supports regulatory requirements for record-keeping under FINMA, MiFID II, and PSD2 strong customer authentication (SCA).

üá®üá≠

Swiss & EU Framework Compatibility

AlpenSign's two-channel authorization model aligns with PSD2/SCA requirements for independent authentication. The architecture is compatible with Switzerland's ZertES (Federal Act on Electronic Signatures) and the broader European eIDAS trust services framework for advanced electronic signatures.
